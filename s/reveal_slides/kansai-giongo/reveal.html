The Question

  • How true are commonly held beliefs about onomatopoeic frequency in Japanese speech ?

  • Do Kinki region speakers really use significantly more onomatopoeia ?

    • Preliminary Study

      Asked 1100 people from 11 regions:

    • How do you use onomatopoeia?
    • Which region uses the most?

      • Results:

    • No significant differences in subjective use
    • Kinki region residents are said to use the most onomatopoeia


    Corpus

    • Minutes from the national legistlature, the Diet
    • Various committes, both houses, meetings: (http://kokkai.ndl.go.jp/)
    • Size: 48.5 million sentences, 3.44 billion characters
    • Speakers: +1000 utterances & have a Japanese hometown
    • Time: 1992 to 2012

    Method

    • Onomatopoeia dictionary
    • Searched for occurrences + と (to) particle
    • 7 categories of onomatopoeias
    • Speakers hometowns: 3,606 of 5,803 people (62.12%)
    • 11 region categories

    Method

    Heat Mapping by Frequency

      In each prefecture:
    • # of sentences with onomatopoeia / total # of sentences


      • For each person:
    • # of sentences with onomatopoeia / total # of sentences
    • Average values for each prefecture were normalized with the maximum

    Conclusion

    The folk dialectology that states Kinki region speakers use more onomatopoeia is:

    • commonly believed
    • partially true...
    • Kinki region speakers certainly use more emphatic onomatopoeia than other parts of Japan

    Conclusion

    • Results confirm another study of local TV programs in the Kinki region regarding onomatopoeia use
    • The folk dialectology is maybe based on the observance of a certain type
    • Wright (2012) shows patterned distributions of phonaestheme frequency by region in the UK
    • Hirata-Mogi et al. claim that "there is larger impact in Asian languages of the relationship between onomatopoeia and dialects or certain areas".
